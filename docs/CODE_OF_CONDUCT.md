# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

**Contributors are expected to:**

- **Be Respectful**: Treat others with dignity, respect, and empathy. Value diverse perspectives and experiences.
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome newcomers and help them learn our community norms and practices.
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together constructively and listen to different perspectives and ideas.
- **Be Constructive**: Provide helpful feedback that improves the project and community.
- **Be Professional**: Maintain a professional and courteous demeanor in all interactions.
- **Be Accountable**: Take responsibility for your words and actions, and learn from mistakes.

**In our community spaces (GitHub, Discord, etc.):**

- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Be respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accept constructive criticism
- Focus on what is best for the community
- Show empathy towards other community members

### Unacceptable Behavior

**The following behaviors are not tolerated:**

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, including sexual harassment, bullying, intimidation, or stalking
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory jokes, language, or actions based on personal characteristics
- **Disrespect**: Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning comments
- **Spam**: Unwanted content, repeated off-topic posts, or promotional content
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing private information about others without consent
- **Threats**: Threats of violence or any form of intimidation
- **Sabotage**: Deliberately disrupting discussions, damaging the project, or undermining others' work
- **Misrepresentation**: Impersonating others or misrepresenting affiliations

**Specific examples include:**

- Using sexualized language or imagery
- Making offensive comments about a person's identity or background
- Publishing private communications without permission
- Deliberately posting false or misleading information
- Abusing the reporting system to harass others
- Creating multiple accounts to bypass moderation

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Official communication channels (Discord, Slack, etc.)
- Public and private meetings related to the project
- Project-related social media accounts
- Private communications when they relate to project participation

This code applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it through the following channels:

**Primary Contact:**
- **Email**: [security@agentchat.app](mailto:security@agentchat.app)
- **GitHub**: Create a private issue with "Code of Conduct" in the title
- **Direct Message**: Contact any project maintainer directly

**Alternative Contacts:**
- Create a private issue on GitHub with "Code of Conduct" in the title
- Contact any project maintainer directly
- Use the reporting form on our website (if available)

**What to Include in Your Report:**
- Your contact information (optional - you can report anonymously)
- Description of the incident
- Individuals involved
- Location where it occurred
- Any supporting evidence (screenshots, links, etc.)
- Desired outcome

### Investigation Process

1. **Immediate Response**: We will acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: We will conduct a thorough and fair investigation
3. **Action**: We will take appropriate action based on findings
4. **Follow-up**: We will communicate actions taken (within privacy constraints)

### Consequences

Unacceptable behavior may result in:

- **Warning**: Private warning about behavior and expectations
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from community spaces
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from all project spaces
- **Project Exclusion**: Removal from contributor status
- **Legal Action**: Involvement of law enforcement for serious violations

**Factors considered in consequences:**
- Severity of the behavior
- Pattern of behavior
- Impact on the community
- Prior warnings
- Cooperation with investigation

## Conflict Resolution

### Minor Disagreements

For minor disagreements or misunderstandings:

1. **Direct Communication**: Try to resolve directly and respectfully
2. **Mediation**: Request help from a community maintainer
3. **Cooling Off**: Take time to cool off if emotions are high

### Major Conflicts

For serious conflicts or repeated issues:

1. **Formal Report**: File a formal Code of Conduct report
2. **Mediation**: We will provide mediation if requested
3. **Escalation**: Serious issues will be handled according to enforcement procedures

## Community Guidelines

### Communication Guidelines

**Be Mindful:**
- Consider cultural and language differences
- Assume good intent from others
- Take time to understand different perspectives
- Remember that text lacks tone and context

**Be Clear:**
- Use clear, unambiguous language
- Provide context when necessary
- Ask for clarification if unsure
- Avoid sarcasm and humor that could be misunderstood

**Be Patient:**
- Allow time for others to respond
- Understand that people have different schedules
- Be patient with newcomers learning the norms
- Recognize that not everyone has English as their first language

### Contribution Guidelines

**When Contributing:**
- Follow the project's contribution guidelines
- Provide clear descriptions of changes
- Be responsive to feedback and reviews
- Help others understand your contributions

**When Reviewing:**
- Be constructive and specific in feedback
- Focus on the code, not the person
- Explain the reasoning behind suggestions
- Recognize the effort put into contributions

## Reporting Mechanisms

### Confidential Reporting

For sensitive reports, you can:
- Email the project maintainers privately
- Use GitHub's private reporting feature
- Contact the Code of Conduct committee

### Non-Confidential Reporting

For less sensitive issues:
- Public post in the appropriate community forum
- Tag maintainers for attention
- Use the reporting tools available

## Enforcement Timeline

- **Initial Response**: Within 24 hours of report
- **Investigation**: Within 72 hours of initial response
- **Resolution**: Within 7 days for most issues
- **Follow-up**: Within 30 days to ensure resolution effectiveness

## Appeals Process

If you believe an enforcement decision was incorrect:

1. **Submit Appeal**: Send a detailed appeal to the maintainers
2. **Review**: Different maintainers will review the case
3. **Decision**: Final decision will be made within 7 days
4. **Implementation**: Decision will be implemented immediately
